Where I come from

I am an Eastern Orthodox Christian, and that shapes how I read Scripture, how I understand the Fathers, and how I approach debate in general. I am not writing as a generic Protestant apologist and I am not interested in defending positions the Orthodox Church itself does not hold. If a topic touches on tradition, worship, or the sacraments, expect the answer to come from that place specifically.

What I actually debate

Most people who come looking for a fight expect apologetics: contradictions, prophecy, textual criticism. I will engage with that, and this site exists partly to give solid answers to the objections that come up most often. But it is not where my interest lives.

What I care about is metaphysics. The existence and nature of God, the coherence of classical theism, the problem of evil, free will and divine foreknowledge, the essence and energies distinction, the grounding of moral realism. These are the conversations I find myself returning to, because they are usually where the real disagreement is hiding underneath the surface level dispute.

Comparative religion polemics, in particular, hold very little interest for me. I would rather spend an hour on whether a contingent universe requires a necessary cause than on why one tradition's holy book is supposedly worse than another's.

How I try to argue

  • I read the strongest form of an objection before I answer it, not the version that is easiest to knock down.
  • I try to say when a passage is genuinely difficult rather than smoothing every rough edge for the sake of winning.
  • I care more about whether an argument is valid and sound than about who it embarrasses.
  • I would rather be corrected on something small than be vague about something that matters.

Why this site exists

Most contradiction lists circulating online repeat the same dozen or so objections without engaging the context, the genre, or the original language. I wanted a place where those specific claims get a real answer, and where the deeper conversation, the one about whether God exists and what kind of God he would have to be, gets equal space. That is Live Orthodox.
