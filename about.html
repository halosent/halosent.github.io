Where I come from

I am an Eastern Orthodox Christian, and that shapes how I read Scripture, how I understand the Fathers, and how I approach debate in general. I am not writing as a generic Protestant apologist and I am not interested in defending positions the Orthodox Church itself does not hold. If a topic touches on tradition, worship, or the sacraments, expect the answer to come from that place specifically.

What I actually debate

Most people who come here are expecting classic apologetics, contradictions, prophecy, textual criticism, and I'm glad to engage with that. Part of why this site exists is to give solid, honest answers to the objections that come up most often. But it's not the only thing I care about, and often not even the main thing.

What interests me most is metaphysics. The existence and nature of God, the coherence of classical theism, the problem of evil, free will and divine foreknowledge, the essence and energies distinction, the grounding of moral realism. These are the conversations I keep coming back to, because they're often where the real disagreement is hiding underneath the surface level dispute.

Comparative religion polemics hold less interest for me personally, though I won't shy away from them if that's where a conversation naturally goes. Given the choice, I'd rather spend an hour on whether a contingent universe requires a necessary cause than on ranking traditions against each other.

How I try to argue

  • I try to engage with the strongest version of an argument, not the easiest one to knock down.
  • I'll say plainly when a passage is genuinely difficult, rather than pretending every question has a tidy answer.
  • I care about whether an argument actually holds up, not about winning or losing.
  • If I'm wrong about something, I'd rather be corrected than stay vague to avoid it.

Why this site exists

Most contradiction lists circulating online repeat the same dozen or so objections without engaging the context, the genre, or the original language. I wanted a place where those specific claims get a real answer, and where the deeper conversation, the one about whether God exists and what kind of God he would have to be, gets equal space.

None of this is about being right for the sake of it. If you disagree with something here, I'd genuinely like to hear why, and I'll do my best to engage with it fairly rather than defensively. This site and the community around it are open to anyone thinking seriously about these questions, whatever you currently believe, including if you're just curious or still working things out. You don't need to already agree with me to belong here.
