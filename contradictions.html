New Testament

Did Judas Hang Himself or Fall Headlong? (Matthew 27 vs. Acts 1)

“Matthew says Judas hanged himself; Acts says he fell and burst open. These accounts cannot both be true.”

The two accounts describe different moments and are not mutually exclusive: a hanging that led to a fall is a natural, not contradictory, reading.

Matthew 27:3-8Acts 1:16-19
Historical Claims

Did the Church Suppress Science? The Galileo Affair Reconsidered

“The Galileo affair proves that Christianity is fundamentally opposed to science and has historically suppressed scientific progress.”

The Galileo affair was a specific, complicated conflict involving politics, personalities, and unproven claims — not a representative clash between Christianity and science, and Orthodox Christianity was not party to it at all.

Philosophical Objections

The Problem of Evil: Can a Good and Powerful God Coexist With Suffering?

“If God is all-good and all-powerful, evil should not exist. Since evil exists, God either is not all-good, not all-powerful, or does not exist.”

Orthodox theology distinguishes the logical and evidential forms of the problem, grounding its response in free will, the goodness of creation, and the cross rather than a tidy theoretical solution.

Genesis 3Job 1-42Romans 8:18-23
Old Testament

Two Creation Accounts in Genesis: A Real Contradiction?

“Genesis 1 and Genesis 2 present two different, conflicting orders of creation.”

Genesis 1 and 2 are complementary in genre and purpose, not competing chronological reports: the second account zooms in on humanity rather than re-narrating a sequence.

Genesis 1:1-2:3Genesis 2:4-25
