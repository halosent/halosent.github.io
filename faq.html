Frequently Asked Questions

Why Eastern Orthodoxy, who's behind this, and how the site actually works.

Why Eastern Orthodoxy specifically

Because it's the tradition that traces its worship, its councils, and its understanding of the Fathers in an unbroken line back to the early Church, without the later additions and departures that split off Catholicism and then Protestantism after it. That's not a knock on either, it's just where the reasoning actually led. Everything on this site, from how contradictions get read to how the Trinity gets explained, comes out of that specific tradition rather than a generic "mere Christianity."

Who runs this site

Live Orthodox is maintained by Unified, an Eastern Orthodox Christian. Contradictions get answered here, but the bigger interest is metaphysics, the existence and nature of God, free will, the problem of evil, that kind of thing. Comparative religion polemics get less attention, not because they don't matter, but because they're not where the site's energy goes.

Do I need to agree with you to be here

No. This site and the Discord around it are open to anyone thinking seriously about these questions, whatever you currently believe, including if you're not sure what you believe yet. Disagreement is genuinely welcome, not just tolerated. If you think an essay gets something wrong, that's exactly the kind of message worth sending.

Why metaphysics instead of just contradictions

Most online contradiction lists repeat the same dozen objections without engaging the context, the genre, or the original language, and answering those is worth doing well. But underneath most of those disputes sits a deeper disagreement about whether God exists at all, and what kind of God he'd have to be. That's usually where the real argument is hiding, so it gets equal space here rather than an afterthought.

What Bible translation do the essays use

Essays reference whichever wording best represents the underlying Hebrew or Greek for a given point, and note it when translation choice actually matters to the argument. The verse lookup tool on the Verses page uses the World English Bible, a modern public domain translation, mainly so it can be looked up freely without licensing restrictions.

Can I suggest a topic or objection

Yes, that's most of what the contact inbox is for. Send whatever objection, question, or correction you've got, and it gets read personally. There's no promise every suggestion turns into an essay, but it's genuinely how most of the current ones got picked.

Is this Unified's personal opinion or official Church teaching

Where an essay states Orthodox doctrine, the Trinity, essence and energies, that kind of thing, it's representing the Church's actual teaching, not a personal spin on it. Where an essay is working through a harder, more contested question, like a specific harmonization of two passages, that's a considered position rather than dogma, and reasonable Orthodox Christians could land somewhere slightly different on the details.

What's the Discord server for

It's where the same questions covered in the essays get argued out live, in real time, with actual back and forth. There's a dedicated space for contradictions, one for metaphysics, one for Trinity and theology, plus general chat and voice channels. You can join from the banner on the homepage or the link on the Contact page.
